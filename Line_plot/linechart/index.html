As expected, the values on the logarithmic scale are much closer to each other. Furthermore, the range of values is much smaller.

First and foremost, logarithmic scales are more useful than linear scales when investigating variables that are exponential, or have large value differences between data points due to some other circumstance. For examlpe, GDP or bacteria growth is often shown in logarithmic scales as the absolute difference are extremely large, making the linear scale less intelligable.
